HOOK: Most hormone imbalances have an inflammation layer underneath. CAPTION: Cortisol dysregulation. Estrogen dominance. Thyroid sluggishness. Irregular cycles. Almost all of them have something in common: Chronic low-grade inflammation. And chronic inflammation often has something underneath it: Oxidative stress. Free radicals are unstable molecules that damage cells — including the cells that produce and regulate your hormones. Antioxidants neutralize free radicals. Molecular hydrogen (H₂) is one of the most selective antioxidants studied to date. I'm not saying water is medicine. I'm saying the foundation matters. Weave F — Women Weren't Built for a 7-Day Week

Only the CTA line changed

Weave F
Format: Reel — a little more educational/provocative  ·  Visual: Calendar graphic with cycle overlay
HOOK: Men have a 24-hour hormonal cycle. Women have a 28-day one. CAPTION: This is not a flaw. It's a feature. Men's hormones roughly reset every 24 hours — so the 9-to-5, five-days-a-week structure was literally designed around their biology. Women operate on a 28-day infradian rhythm. That means: the same task, done on Day 5 versus Day 22, will feel completely different — not because your discipline changed, but because your hormones did. When you know this, everything makes more sense: — Why some weeks you're unstoppable — Why some weeks you just can't — Why "just be consistent" advice never quite fits You ARE consistent. Just on a longer cycle. Start tracking yours. ✦ CTA: Free interactive tracker in bio — takes 30 seconds to set up.
