Chapter Two The Emotions You Were Told Were Too Much

From the manuscript. Full page. Let it land.

Explains how children suppress emotions when those emotions weren't welcomed or safe. The "performing" self vs. the real self. Key message: You didn't become fake — you became strategic. That was smart. You get to choose differently now.

  1. Think of a feeling that's present right now. Not the story around it — just the sensation.
  2. Where do you feel it in your body? Put a hand there.
  3. Give it a shape. A color. A texture.
  4. Watch it the way you'd watch a wave. You don't have to stop it. You don't have to ride it. Just... watch.
  5. Notice: waves crest. They always come down. So does this.
Teaching tolerating emotion without suppressing or being overwhelmed by it is the core of somatic healing work. This practice builds "window of tolerance."

Teaching: The patterns that kept you safe then — fawning, over-explaining, shrinking, controlling — were adaptive. They cost you something. Key message: Your coping strategies are not your character.

Somatic — "Softening the Armor": Roll shoulders back and down. Feel the chest open. Take 3 breaths into the chest. Notice what you were protecting by closing there.

Game — Fill-in: "The strategy I used most to stay safe was ___. It worked because ___. What it cost me was ___. I'm ready to thank it by saying: ___."

Which survival strategy shows up most in your life right now?
If that pattern had a voice, what would it say it's trying to protect?

Mantra: "I survived. And survival is not the ceiling — it's the floor I get to build from."

Teaching: Triggers are old memories looking for an update. Your nervous system is pattern-matching to the past. Key message: You are not overreacting. You are under-informed — your body hasn't gotten the news yet that it's safe.

Somatic — "Pendulation": Notice a mild activation in the body. Then shift attention to something neutral or pleasant in the room. Back to the activation. Back to neutral. Repeat. This teaches the nervous system it can move between states.

Game — I Spy (Then vs Now): "I spy something that belongs to NOW, not then." Find 3 things in the room that are proof you're not in the past. Write them. "These are evidence."

When you notice yourself triggered, what does your body do first — before your mind catches up?
What would you want to say to the version of you who first learned that response?

Mantra: "My past was real. My body remembers. And right now, in this moment, I am safe enough to heal."

Teaching: What inner child work actually is — not regression, not "becoming" a child. It's building a relationship with the part of you that stopped developing when it got scared. Key message: She didn't go anywhere. She went quiet.

Somatic — "Hand on Heart": Place one or both hands on the center of your chest. Feel the warmth of your own hands. Let yourself be held — by yourself. Breathe into your hands. Say (silently or aloud): "I see you. I'm not leaving."

Game — Word Search: Hidden words: PLAY · MUD · STARS · NAPS · STORIES · WONDER · CRAYON · MAGIC · LAUGH · CLIMB · DREAM · SILLY. Caption: "She remembers all of these."

Art Page: Draw yourself at age 7. What are you doing? Where are you? What's on your face?

Mantra: "She has been waiting patiently. I'm showing up now."

Teaching: The stories we tell about ourselves formed in childhood to make sense of things that didn't make sense. They were the best explanations a small person could form. You are the author now.

Somatic — "Shaking It Out": Stand up if you can. Gently shake your hands, then wrists, then arms, then whole body. 60 seconds. This is how animals discharge stress after a threat. Let it be weird. Let it work.

Game — Mad Libs Affirmation: Fill in the blanks: "I am ___. I used to believe I was ___, but that story belonged to ___. I release ___. The truest thing about me is ___."

Teaching: Most of the rules we follow were absorbed, not chosen. They served someone — often not us. Key message: You are allowed to audit the rules and keep only the ones that are actually yours.

Somatic — "The Long Exhale": Breathe in for 4 counts. Hold for 2. Exhale for 8. Repeat 4 times. The extended exhale activates the vagus nerve — the physical mechanism of letting go.
