What I Didn't Know About Water

Here's something I didn't know until recently: Tap water in the US has an average pH of 7. Filtered water can actually drop BELOW 7 (acidic). Most bottled water sits around 6.5–7. And your body — your blood especially — works hard to stay at 7.35 to 7.45. When your water is acidic, your body has to work overtime just to neutralize it before it can actually hydrate you. The result? You drink plenty of water. But you're still tired, still inflamed, still running on empty. This is the thing nobody talks about. We've been told to "drink more water" but never told what the water itself is doing to us. I'll be sharing more about what I've switched to and why.
