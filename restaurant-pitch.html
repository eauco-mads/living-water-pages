What you're probably

How does it connect to my kitchen? +
The K8 connects directly to your existing water faucet — no plumber, no renovation, no downtime. It installs and uninstalls easily, speaks 7 languages, walks itself through monthly cleaning (set it and forget it), and alerts you if anything needs attention. Every staff member can use it from day one.
Is the pH 2.5 sanitizer actually legal for food service? +
Yes. The FDA, EPA, and USDA have all approved pH 2.5 hypochlorous acid (HOCl) for food handling and food contact surface sanitation. It's the same compound used in hospital sanitation and food processing facilities. It's 100x more effective at killing microbial pathogens than chlorine bleach — and produces no toxic fumes, no residue, and requires no protective equipment.
Does it really replace bleach, detergent, and produce spray? +
Yes. pH 2.5 replaces bleach, disinfecting sprays, vinegar, and Windex. pH 11.5 replaces degreaser, produce cleaning spray, laundry detergent, and floor cleaner. A one-time investment replaces ongoing supply costs across multiple product categories for the 25+ year life of the machine. The net savings across a restaurant are typically in the thousands per year.
What does it actually do to food flavor? +
Tap water contains chlorine and other compounds that food literally absorbs during cooking — it alters and restricts natural flavor. Kangen water at 8.5–9.5 pH has no chlorine, and its smaller molecular cluster size means it penetrates food more readily, extracting more of the natural aromatic compounds. Water also boils faster at alkaline pH. Multiple restaurateurs describe doing a blind side-by-side on day one and immediately noticing the difference in the same dish.
What does maintenance actually look like? +
Filter changes 1–2x per year (the machine tells you when) at $100–135 each. E-cleaning every 3–4 weeks: the machine runs a 3-hour overnight cleaning cycle automatically, requiring ~60g of food-grade citric acid (~$25 on Amazon, lasts 2+ years). Annual professional deep cleaning is available. Electrolysis enhancer for producing 2.5 and 11.5 pH waters runs ~$75–150/year. Total maintenance: well under $500/year for a machine that replaces thousands in supplies.
What's the investment and can I write it off? +
Machine investment ranges from $5,000–7,300 depending on model. Yes — as a business appliance directly used in your restaurant operations, it qualifies as a tax write-off. Combined with the supply cost savings and new income from memberships and distributor commissions, most restaurants see the machine pay for itself within the first year or two.
