Day 2 · Education

What I Didn't Know About Water

Here's something I didn't know until recently: Tap water in the US has an average pH of 7. Filtered water can actually drop BELOW 7 (acidic). Most bottled water sits around 6.5–7. And your body — your blood especially — works hard to stay at 7.35 to 7.45. When your water is acidic, your body has to work overtime just to neutralize it before it can actually hydrate you. The result? You drink plenty of water. But you're still tired, still inflamed, still running on empty. This is the thing nobody talks about. We've been told to "drink more water" but never told what the water itself is doing to us. I'll be sharing more about what I've switched to and why. Stay tuned. 💧 Drop a 💧 if you want to learn more about what you're actually drinking.

Day 6 · Objection Handling

"Isn't All Water Just Water?"

I used to think that too. Honest. "Water is water. H₂O. How different can it be?" Here's what changed my mind: You wouldn't say "food is food" — a bag of chips and a salmon fillet are both food, but what they do to your body is completely different. Water has a structure. It has a pH. It carries dissolved minerals and chemicals and byproducts of treatment processes. The water that comes out of your tap was engineered to not make you immediately sick — not to help your cells function optimally. The difference between "not harmful" and "actively beneficial" is huge. And most people are spending their whole lives in the "not harmful" zone, wondering why they still feel off. I'm not saying tap water is poison. I'm saying there's a better version. And once you feel the difference, you can't unfeel it. Have you ever felt different drinking water from certain places? Some spring, some city, some well? Comment below — I want to hear it. 👇
