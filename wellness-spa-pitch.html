What is molecular hydrogen and what does it actually do? +
Molecular hydrogen (H₂) is the smallest molecule in existence — small enough to cross into every cell, including through the blood-brain barrier. It acts as a selective antioxidant, targeting only the most harmful free radicals (hydroxyl radicals) without disrupting beneficial oxidative processes. Peer-reviewed studies link it to reduced inflammation, faster tissue recovery, improved cellular function, and neuroprotection. For post-procedure clients, the anti-inflammatory mechanism is especially relevant.

See the research →
Why pH 6.0 after botox or laser — what's the mechanism? +
Healthy skin has a natural pH between 4.5 and 6.5. This is called the acid mantle — a thin protective layer that keeps bacteria out and moisture in. Most tap water is pH 7.0–8.5, which disrupts this barrier, increasing sensitivity and transepidermal water loss. pH 6.0 beauty water matches the skin's natural environment exactly. Applied post-procedure, it helps the skin rebalance without additional products or chemical exposure, soothes irritation, and closes pores tightened by treatment.
Is the pH 2.5 sanitation water safe for a clinical setting? +
Yes. Electrolyzed oxidizing water at pH 2.5 (containing hypochlorous acid, HOCl) is recognized by the FDA and EPA as safe and effective for surface disinfection. It kills bacteria, viruses, and fungi on contact — in under 30 seconds. It's used in hospitals, dental offices, food processing facilities, and medical clinics. Unlike bleach or quaternary ammonium compounds, it leaves no chemical residue, has no fumes, and is safe for skin contact. It reverts to regular water within hours of production.
What makes this different from filtered or alkaline water at the store? +
Bottled alkaline water adds minerals to raise the pH — it doesn't contain molecular hydrogen (H₂), which is what drives the therapeutic benefit. The Kangen K8 uses electrolysis to restructure water at the molecular level, creating H₂-rich water with a negative ORP (Oxidation Reduction Potential). Store-bought alkaline water has a positive ORP — meaning it acts as an oxidant rather than an antioxidant. The difference is the process, not just the pH number. This is the same distinction between synthetic and bioavailable supplements.
